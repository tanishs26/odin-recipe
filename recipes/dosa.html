Masala Dosa

Masala Dosa is a popular South Indian dish made by stuffing a crispy, thin rice crepe with a spiced potato filling. It is usually served with coconut chutney and sambar.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black gram lentils)
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • 3-4 medium-sized potatoes
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for cooking

Steps:

  1. Wash and soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind soaked rice to a smooth batter consistency. Similarly, grind soaked urad dal and fenugreek seeds into a smooth batter.
  3. Mix both batters together and let it ferment overnight or for 8-10 hours.
  4. Boil and mash potatoes. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and let them splutter.
  5. Add chopped onions, green chilies, and saut√© until onions turn golden brown.
  6. Add turmeric powder, mashed potatoes, salt, and mix well. Cook for a few minutes until the flavors blend.
  7. To make dosas, heat a non-stick pan, pour a ladleful of batter, and spread it thinly in a circular motion.
  8. Drizzle oil around the edges and cook until the dosa turns golden and crisp.
  9. Place a portion of the potato filling in the center of the dosa, fold it, and serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
